The line graph shows the GDP growth trend in the past 62 years using current US$ of the top 10 countries in 2021.The top 10 GDP countries in 2021 are Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Italy, Japan, Korea, United Kingdom and United States.

Among the top ten countries in terms of global GDP, it can be clearly seen that the United States is far ahead all the way, and Japan has grown significantly in the early 1990s and has remained second in the world. The Asian financial crisis at the end of the last century has caused Japan's GDP to stagnate until now. At the same time, with China's reform and opening up, China's GDP began to maintain rapid growth around 2000, rapidly surpassing other countries' GDP. Around 2010, China's GDP surpassed Japan's, ranking second in the world, and gradually approaching the United States. Other countries maintained steady growth, and the GDP of China and the United States far exceeded that of other countries.
